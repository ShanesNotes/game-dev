The concept you've secretly already used

Back in Lesson 4, when you made the Sprite2D and CollisionShape2D children of Player, they started moving with the player automatically. You never wrote code to move them. That's the rule:

◈ parent-child transform

A child node's position is relative to its parent. Move the parent, and every child moves with it — for free. It's one of the most useful ideas in the whole engine.

A Camera2D decides which slice of the world you see. So if we make the camera a child of the Player… it follows the player everywhere, no code required. That's the whole trick.

First: give yourself room to scroll

A camera that follows is invisible if the whole map fits on screen. So let's make the world bigger.

1

Select your Ground tilemap. Using the tile palette from Lesson 5, paint a lot more grass — extend the floor well past the edges of the blue camera rectangle, in every direction. Make it big.

2

Zoom out in the editor (Ctrl + scroll, or −) if you need to see what you're painting.

Add the camera

1

Right-click Player → Add Child Node → search Camera2D → Create. It must be a child of Player, not Main.

2

That's it. A Camera2D automatically becomes the active camera, and because it's parented to Player, it inherits the player's movement.

3

Press F5 and walk around. The robot stays centred while the grass scrolls past. 🎉

Optional juice: smooth the follow

Right now the camera is glued rigidly to the robot. A tiny bit of lag feels much nicer.

1

Select the Camera2D. In the Inspector, open Position Smoothing and tick Enabled.

2

Run again. The camera now eases toward the robot — a subtle drift that makes movement feel alive. Bump the Speed value up or down to taste.

Check yourself
