How rage works in WoW

Unlike mana, you don't start with rage — you earn it by fighting. Every blow you land (and every one you take) adds rage, scaled by a level-based conversion value. It caps at 100 and decays when you're out of combat.

Conversion value (C)

A number that grows with your level (reference §8). It's the divisor in the rage formula — small at low level, so you make lots of rage early.

Dealing damage

7.5 × dmg / C + (f × speed) / 2, where f is 3.5 (normal) or 7.0 (crit).

Taking damage

2.5 × dmg / C — getting hit makes you angrier too.

Part B — earn rage from the fight

Add the conversion value and three small helpers. add_rage is the one place that changes rage and updates the bar:

GDScript · player.gd
func conversion_value():
    return 0.0091107836 * level * level + 3.225598133 * level + 4.2652911

func add_rage(amount):
    rage = clampf(rage + amount, 0, max_rage)
    rage_bar.value = rage

func gain_rage_dealing(damage, is_crit):
    var f = 7.0 if is_crit else 3.5
    add_rage(7.5 * damage / conversion_value() + f * weapon_speed / 2.0)

func gain_rage_taking(damage):
    add_rage(2.5 * damage / conversion_value())

Now call them. A landed blow gives rage (misses/dodges/parries don't), so capture the damage in attack():

GDScript · player.gd — attack() landing branches
    elif roll < miss + dodge + parry + glancing:
        spawn_text_over(t, "Glancing", Color.GRAY)
        var dmg = roundi(glancing_damage(t))
        t.take_damage(dmg)
        gain_rage_dealing(dmg, false)
    elif roll < miss + dodge + parry + glancing + crit:
        var dmg = attack_damage * 2
        t.take_damage(dmg, true)
        gain_rage_dealing(dmg, true)
    else:
        t.take_damage(attack_damage)
        gain_rage_dealing(attack_damage, false)

And earn rage when bitten — one line in take_damage:

GDScript · player.gd — take_damage
    health_bar.value = health
    spawn_number(amount, Color.RED)
    gain_rage_taking(amount)      # getting hit builds rage too
add_rage is the single chokepoint — every change clamps to 0–100 and refreshes the bar. Funnel all rage changes through it.
Only landed blows generate rage. The miss/dodge/parry branches don't call gain_rage_dealing — whiffs make no rage, just like WoW.

Part C — rage drains out of combat

When you're not swinging, rage bleeds away. Add this to the end of _physics_process:

GDScript · player.gd — in _physics_process
    if swing_timer.is_stopped() and rage > 0:
        add_rage(-rage_decay * delta)   # out of combat -> drain
swing_timer.is_stopped() — our simple "out of combat" check. While you're auto-attacking the timer runs (no drain); when the fight ends it stops and rage bleeds down.
-rage_decay * delta — frame-rate independent: ~1 rage/second no matter the FPS (delta is seconds-per-frame).
